Short Autobiography 

                    My name is Ákos Tibor Hadar. I was born in 2005 in the Hungarian Transcarpathian village of 
                    Csongor (pronounced 'Chongor'). I spent the bulk of my childhood there with most of my family 
                    until 2013, at which point my father moved to London and my mother to Budapest. After 3 more 
                    years with my grandparents, I joined my father in May of 2016. As I had no English knowledge at 
                    the time, I spent my first Summer learning the language from one of our flatmates and now very 
                    close friends. Even today, almost 10 years later, I would describe that Summer as the best one 
                    I've had so far. 

                    At the end of the Summer, I joined The Crest Academy in Brent, London where I completed eight 
                    GCSEs with the majority of my final grades being 8s and 9s. Having completed my GCSEs, I then started A-levels in Guildford 
                    County School studying Physics, Maths, Further Maths and Computer Science. While there, I took part in the UKMT Senior 
                    Mathematical Challenge of 2022, recieving a Gold Certificate, and also received the Gold Duke of Edinburgh Award. 

                    In the Summer of 2023, I, my father and another close friend of ours embarked on a cycling trip from London to Budapest, 
                    setting off on the 2nd and arriving to the infamous 'Square of Heroes' (Hősök Tere) on the 15th of July. Just 2 months later 
                    in September, I started an Integrated Master's course in Mathematics and Physics (MPhys) at the University of Surrey where I
                    am currently averaging a First Class. 

                    Since my arrival to the University, I have taken part in the 2025 International Physics Tournament hosted by IOP, representing 
                    the UK through Surrey. (the paper our team produced is available to read on the 'Projects' page). I have also worked as a Re-
                    search Assistant for 2 months in the Physics Department at the University of Oxford in the context of a paid internship, looking 
                    at the use of reduced-precision arithmetic, stochastic physics and machine learning in weather forecasting.

Future Goals
